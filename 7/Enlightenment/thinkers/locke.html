Background

John Locke was an English philosopher during the Enlightenment who focused on the structure of governments. Locke believed that man was naturally kind and, when given the choice, will help others before helping himself. Since man is naturally good-at-heart, Locke believed that a strict government was not necessary; according to Locke, people would work out problems by openly compromising with one another.

Two Treatises of Government (1690)

In his famous book on government, Two Treatises of Government (1690), Locke wrote about the relationship between government and its people.
