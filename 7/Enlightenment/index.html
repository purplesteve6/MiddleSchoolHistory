What Was the Enlightenment?

The Enlightenment was an intellectual movement that spread across Europe in the 1600s and 1700s. Thinkers believed society could improve by using reason instead of relying only on tradition or authority.

Some Enlightenment writers used pen names or wrote secretly because criticizing kings or churches could lead to censorship‚Äîor even prison.

Core Ideas of the Enlightenment

1. Reason
Human reason is the best tool for understanding the world.
Logic > blind faith or inherited tradition.
2. Science & the Scientific Method
Knowledge should come from observation and experimentation.
Built on thinkers like Isaac Newton, even if he focused more on science than philosophy.
3. Natural Rights
People are born with rights like life, liberty, and property.
Governments exist to protect those rights‚Äînot take them away.
4. Progress
Society can improve through education, reform, and rational thinking.
History does not have to repeat itself forever.
5. Skepticism of Authority
Kings, churches, and governments should not be obeyed just because they have power.
Authority must be justified.
