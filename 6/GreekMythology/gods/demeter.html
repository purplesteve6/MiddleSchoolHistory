Background

Demeter is the goddess of agriculture, fertility, and the harvest. She is deeply connected to the cycle of life and nature, ensuring that crops grow and seasons change. Her most famous myth revolves around the abduction of her daughter Persephone, which helped the ancient Greeks explain why the world turns green in spring and summer but grows cold and barren in fall and winter. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Symbols associated with Demeter include wheat, the cornucopia, and the torch. She was widely worshiped across Greece, especially through the Eleusinian Mysteries, a sacred festival and set of rites that focused on life, death, and rebirth. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Demeter is sometimes shown as a calm, powerful mother figure‚Äîbecause so many of her myths center around caring for Persephone. But she is also a force of nature. When Demeter is satisfied, fields are full and people are fed. When she grieves, the land can become empty and harsh.
