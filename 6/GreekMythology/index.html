Where Do the Gods Come From?

Greek mythology begins with stories about how the world started. Early myths describe powerful forces and beings that came before the Olympians. These stories help explain how the Greeks imagined order forming out of chaos.

Gaia: The Earth

Gaia represents the Earth. In many Greek creation stories, she is one of the first beings and becomes the ‚Äúmother‚Äù of many important figures in myth.

Cronus and the Titans

The Titans were powerful beings who ruled before the Olympians. Cronus is one of the most famous Titans, and his story helps set up the conflict between the old rulers and the new gods.

Cyclopes and Mythic Creatures

Greek myths also include strange and powerful creatures. Stories about Cyclopes and other beings highlight how the Greeks imagined danger, strength, and the unknown.
